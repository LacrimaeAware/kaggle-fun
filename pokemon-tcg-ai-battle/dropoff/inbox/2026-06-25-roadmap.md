## Roadmap (the broad goal — all of it)

### 1. Search: better opponent-response emulation  [USER: "one of the easier wins"]
The bug: `search_v3._search` defaults the opponent's deck to OUR deck (`op_src = opp_prior or deck`), so it
determinizes the opponent's hidden cards from our own list. Fix tiers (do in order):
- [NEXT] Bare minimum: stop assuming our deck. Use the replay DB to guess the opponent's decklist
  (`tools/build_opponent_meta_v1.py` -> `data/opponent_meta_v1.json`; pass `opp_decks`/`opp_weights` to
  `best_option`). Also never seed the opponent pool with cards only in our list.
- [NEXT] After emulating the opponent's hand, assume option-0 for their reply as the cheap baseline (the
  current rollout already roughly does best-attack; option-0 is the floor).
- [LATER] Use the rich-feature next-move predictor to pick the opponent's likely reply in the rollout
  (imitation caveat: it predicts the recorded move, not the winning one).
- [LATER] Average response AND worst-case across many sims (we already average over determinizations; add a
  min/worst-case aggregation for safety).
Note: the meta-opponent A/B was "neutral" locally, but that was mirror self-play which cannot show opponent
modeling. This is a ladder-only win; do not dismiss it on local numbers.

### 4. Heuristic fine-tuning + loss diagnosis  [USER: branching-match idea]
- [NEXT, design via workflow] Branching-match diagnosis harness: simulate a match; whenever a chosen heuristic
  is about to fire, branch and play the rest of the match twice (take it vs skip/alternative), record which
  branch won and the scenario (obs snapshot), and save divergent scenarios for review of WHY the heuristic
  helped or hurt (human review best; AI can triage). This is how we find the situations the agent loses and
  fix the responsible heuristic, instead of guessing.
- [NEXT] KO-last sequencing in `lethal_ko` (develop non-endangering plays first, attack last) — the one finding
  not yet in the heuristic. Test as a frozen state, then ladder.

  special energy is about to enable an attack next turn; handle Rare Candy skipping an evolution). Mirror can't

## Investigation findings (2026-06-25) + recommended sequence

Concrete facts from the parallel investigation. Recommended order: 1) opponent A/B + wire, 2) Starmie pilot,
3) branch-diag harness, 4) corpus refresh, 5) learned predictor (last).

- **Replay corpus:** `pokemon-ai-agent/data/external/replays/` — 4501 game JSONs, 17 GB, gitignored, single copy
  (Desktop checkout only; not in the Codex worktree). Episode-id range 80429575-81447094, fetched 06-22/06-23.
  Refresh: `pokemon-ai-agent/tools/refresh_replay_corpus.py` (episode JSON public-by-id, no auth for fetch).
  FRAGILITY: `build_opponent_meta_v1.py` reads it by hardcoded absolute path; add an env/const override.
- **Opponent modeling [highest leverage, low effort]:** confirmed bug — `search_v3._search` defaults the
  opponent's deck to OUR deck. Fix is plumbed (`opp_decks`/`opp_weights`) and `opponent_meta_v1.json` exists
  (4501 games, 554 decks). Caveat I add: the local meta-opponent A/B is MIRROR self-play, where the opponent
  really does run our deck, so it cannot validate this and may read false-negative; it is a LADDER-only win.
  Wire it into production search (`main.py` `_agent_search`/`agent_planner`), not just the rare fallback.
  (Already wired into `sub_heuristic2`'s fallback; the bigger win is wiring it into the pure-search `sub_planner`.)
- **Decks / Starmie [ceiling-changing]:** shipped Alakazam P001 is **13W-23L** on its 36-game replay slice.
  Starmie archetype = **261/387 = 67.4%** across 13 pilots; keidroid (Cinderace/Starmie) 69.4% over 268 games,
  tomatomato (Froslass/Starmie) 70.5%. Mega Starmie ex (1031): HP330, plain 120/210 attacks (no dynamic text)
  = LOW complexity = good fit for our generic forward-model search. Heuristics are mostly deck-agnostic
  (main.py/search_v3 work via card-stat lookups; only `DECK` is hardcoded; `deck_policy_v3` is Alakazam-specific
  but goes inert/no-op on a foreign deck). Top Starmie deck from the meta (count 387):
  `3x9 17x4 666x4 1030x3 1031x3 1086x4 1097x2 1120x4 1121x1 1122x4 1145x4 1159x1 1182x1 1189x4 1223x2 1225x2 1227x4 1229x4`.
  KEY lesson: the human ~70% is THEIR pilot, not ours — measure our pilot before switching. The gap is usually
  piloting, not the deck.
  **MEASURED (2026-06-25, tools/deck_pilot_ab_v1.py):** our generic pilot on Starmie BEAT our generic pilot on
  Alakazam **33-7 (0.825, n=40, CI [0.71,0.94])** head to head. So the deck is a real limiter for us, not just
  a piloting gap. Caveat: that used the generic pilot on both sides (Alakazam without its heuristics). The fair
  switch test, Starmie-generic vs `heuristic_first` (best Alakazam), is running next; if Starmie still wins,
  switching decks is clearly worth a ladder slot. NEXT after that: pick a canonical Starmie list (keidroid /
  tomatomato exact lists), build a Starmie submission on the generic search path, ladder-test it.
- **Rich-feature predictor (C6) [lowest priority]:** sparse-linear option-ranker (behavior cloning), test top1
  0.507 vs option-0 0.474 (+3 pts), but weak exactly where it matters (attack 0.17, end_turn 0.0). HIGH effort
  to use (needs a live-state -> packed-record adapter), low/uncertain leverage, imitation ceiling. Use only as a
  behavioral prior over the replay population, never argmax-as-best-move. Defer until the branch harness shows a
  prior would change outcomes.
- **Branching-match diagnosis [measurement backbone]:** build `tools/branch_diag_v1.py`. At each real decision
  where a heuristic's pick differs from its alternative, fork under common random numbers (draw hidden zones
  once, play take-fork and skip-fork from the same determinization, R=8-16), record `delta = p_win_take -
  p_win_skip`, the scenario snapshot, and flag sign-flips for human review. Reuses `search_v3` primitives
  (search_begin/step/end). Pitfalls to stamp into each record: it's a determinization (use the meta opponent,
  not our deck) and the rollout is attack-greedy (`_rollout_pick`), so verdicts are "under this rollout policy."
